# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Out of Office's primary audiences, in priority order:

- **Filmmakers and creative collaborators** — editors, DPs, producers, sound. Future crew and co-conspirators on documentary work.
- **AI builders and researchers working in the open** — potential film subjects, sources, and long-term collaborators. The people on the other side of the camera.
- **AI-curious viewers** — would-be audience for the films and the channels. The site exists to imprint, not pitch.

Context when they arrive: cold, often via referral or social link. They're vetting whether OOO is real or marketing, and they read taste signals before information.

## Product Purpose

Out of Office is an independent documentary studio covering the people, ideas, and protocols building artificial intelligence in the open — the decentralized side of the field, not the big-lab corporate story.

The website's job is **imprint**: leave the visitor remembering the name, the framing ("cinema for the decentralized intelligence era"), and the fact that this exists as a real editorial enterprise. Conversion to YouTube/X is a downstream byproduct of recognition, not the primary goal.

Success: a visitor leaves convinced this is a serious independent studio, files the name, and recognizes it next time it appears in their feed.

## Brand Personality

**Urgent. Editorial. Anti-corporate.**

Voice: documentary-grade rigor in the spirit of Vice early era, Topic, Field of Vision, Means TV. Independent journalism's confidence. Willing to be raw, unfinished, or strange when the subject demands it. Never glossy, never copywriter-clever, never SaaS-cheerful.

Emotional goal: the visitor feels they've stumbled onto something underground but disciplined — a publication with a point of view, not a content channel.

**Visual trajectory.** The site today sits at the restrained, monochrome-editorial end of this lane. The direction of travel is *toward* the rougher, denser, more urgent edge of the family — editorial density, asymmetric layout, willingness to be loud when subjects demand it. Future iterations should push that way, not retreat from it.

## Anti-references

OOO must explicitly NOT look like:

- **Crypto / web3 dark mode** — neon-on-black, terminal cosplay, retrofuturism, "building the future" energy. Despite the subject matter, OOO is a film studio, not a protocol.
- **Big Tech AI clinical** — OpenAI / Anthropic / Google AI white-paper aesthetic. Sterile whitespace, muted blue accents, "responsible AI" mood. OOO documents this world; it does not belong to it.
- (Bonus repellents to keep in view: doc-studio cliché — sepia film grain, vignette hero images, voiceover-prose copy. References should be contemporary editorial publications, not "powered by stories" production companies.)

## Design Principles

1. **Imprint over conversion.** Every page choice prioritizes memorability over click-through. Refuse CTAs and engagement patterns that betray the editorial frame.
2. **Documentary-grade, never documentary-stylized.** Borrow the *rigor* of independent journalism, never its visual clichés. Treat the form the way the subjects treat their work: open, on the record, no costume.
3. **Anti-AI-vernacular.** OOO is *about* AI but must not *speak* like AI brands. No "intelligence / synthesis / protocol" word soup. No gradients, glass, or neon. The visual vocabulary is independent print, not foundation-model lab.
4. **Push toward underground confidence.** The Vice / Topic / Field of Vision lineage permits asymmetry, density, weird crops, urgency — and the trajectory should keep moving that way. Rough can be intentional; sloppy never is.
5. **Show, don't tell.** The work is the argument. The site sets the stakes for the films; it does not promote them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA as the floor for all production work.

- Contrast: text against background must meet AA (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large). Current paper / ink palette clears this comfortably.
- Focus rings: visible on all interactive elements; never disabled.
- Motion: honor `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere. Editorial register favors stillness anyway; motion is purposeful, not default.
- Semantics: landmarks, headings in order, descriptive `aria-label` on iconographic or visually-implicit links.
- Keyboard: full keyboard parity with mouse; tab order matches reading order.

No specific user-need constraints to design around at this time.
